Cuisine

Viennese cuisine is famous for its delicious dishes and refined desserts.

  • Wiener Schnitzel

    A breaded veal schnitzel, fried until golden and crispy. It is one of Austria’s most iconic dishes.

  • Apfelstrudel

    A traditional Viennese dessert made of thin dough filled with apples, raisins, cinnamon, and sugar, often served with vanilla sauce.

  • Sachertorte

    A famous Viennese cake consisting of a dense chocolate sponge filled with apricot jam and covered with a rich chocolate glaze.

  • Goulash

    A variation of goulash made with beef, onions, and paprika, offering a rich and flavorful taste

History of the city

Austro-Hungarian Empire

For a long time, Vienna was the capital of the Austro-Hungarian Empire, one of the most influential empires in Europe. The imperial heritage is reflected in the city’s luxurious palaces, museums, and historical buildings.

Mozart

Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart, one of the greatest composers in the world, spent much of his life in Vienna. The city keeps his memory alive through museums, concerts, and monuments dedicated to his work.

Transport

  • Metro (U-Bahn): The metro network (U-Bahn) is one of the fastest ways to travel through the city, covering the main tourist areas and districts.
  • Trams: Trams are symbolic for the city and offer a picturesque way to explore Vienna, while being both practical and comfortable
  • Vienna Pass Card: The Vienna Pass is a tourist card that offers free access to numerous attractions, public transport included, and discounts at various locations. It is ideal for visitors who want to explore as much as possible in a short time.
